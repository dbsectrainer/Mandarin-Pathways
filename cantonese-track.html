📖 Jyutping Romanization 粵拼

Jyutping (粵拼 Jyut6ping3) is the standard romanization system for Cantonese, developed by the Linguistic Society of Hong Kong. Unlike Mandarin's Pinyin, Cantonese has 6 distinct tones.

The 6 Cantonese Tones

Tone Jyutping Number Contour Example Meaning
1 — High level1High flat ˥si1 詩poem
2 — High rising2Rising ˧˥si2 史history
3 — Mid level3Mid flat ˧si3 試to try
4 — Low falling4Falling ˨˩si4 時time
5 — Low rising5Low rising ˩˧si5 市market
6 — Low level6Low flat ˨si6 事matter

Common Cantonese Sentence-Final Particles

Cantonese uses many sentence-final particles that add nuance. These are one of the most distinctive features of the language.

囉 lo3 — so / obviously 喎 wo3 — I hear that / apparently 呀 aa3 — softening / exclamation 嘅 ge3 — that's how it is 咋 zaa3 — only / just 㗎 gaa3 — emphasis/affirmation 囉喎 lo3wo3 — I'm telling you 啩 gwaa3 — probably / I suppose
